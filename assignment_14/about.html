Who is Santa?

Santa Claus, also known as Saint Nicholas, Kris Kringle, or simply Santa, is a legendary figure with a global cultural impact, especially during the festive season of Christmas. Originating from various traditions, Santa has evolved into the iconic, benevolent gift-giver who is said to reside at the North Pole, accompanied by a workshop of industrious elves. Clad in a red suit with white fur trim, Santa is recognized for his hearty laughter and trademark "Ho, ho, ho!" greeting. One of the most beloved and enduring symbols of holiday cheer, Santa is said to deliver gifts to well-behaved children around the world on Christmas Eve, sliding down chimneys and leaving presents under decorated trees. The image of Santa Claus has transcended generations, fostering a spirit of generosity and joy that defines the magic of Christmas for people of all ages.
